Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset

Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasticity is essentially molding the brain through repetition or practice. The more you repeat the task the easier it becomes for you remember. For example it is hard to remember the names of people you just met but it is easier to remember the names of loved ones you haven’t seen in a decade.

How understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people:

Neuroplasticity helps people with disabilities to achieve specific tasks through repetition and practice.

Motivates them to learn new things as they know that they are fully capable of building knowledge and changing their brains.

Engaging with the principles of neuroplasticity for my benefit:

Neuroplasticity can help me benefit by achieving my goals through repetition and practice.

It is clear that if we don’t use something for a long period of time we will forget it.

It also tells us that experience from the plasticity of one skill can help acquire and enhance similar skills.

Repeatedly practicing and reviewing will help me in storing the knowledge in my brain otherwise it will fade and weaken overtime.

Using the skill set learned from one situation and applying it to the other.

Ways to increase your neuroplasticity:

Repeated practicing one skill to strengthen memory i.e., connection of the existing neural pathway.

Putting the information into context in a way that even a kid can understand.

Constantly reminding yourself that anything is achievable through repetition or practice.

Growth Mindset

I believe that with effort, determination, and focus, we can achieve any goal. Small, steady steps are often more helpful than trying to do too much at once.

To overcome obstacles, it's important to be honest about our weaknesses and work on improving them.

As a new programmer, I know that success comes from learning and understanding.

Neuroplasticity and Growth Mindset and how it influences my learning:

Neuroplasticity is like a magic trick for your brain. It means your brain can change and grow, even as you get older. It's like building new muscles – the more you use your brain, the stronger it gets.

Growth mindset is like believing you can learn and improve, no matter how hard things get. It’s like thinking, “I can do this!” even when it’s tough.

Together, neuroplasticity and growth mindset are a powerful combination. They help you learn new things, overcome challenges, and reach your goals.

Example:

Neuroplasticity is like planting new seeds in the garden. It means your brain can grow and learn new things, just like a garden can grow new flowers.

Growth mindset is like believing you can make your garden grow bigger and more beautiful, no matter how small it is now.

Together, neuroplasticity and growth mindset can help you learn like a gardener:
  1. Plant new seeds: Try new things and challenge yourself.
  2. Tend to your garden: Practice regularly and don't give up.
  3. Believe in your garden: Have confidence that you can grow and learn.

By using these tools, you can make your learning journey a success!
