Te Whare Tapa Whā

Te Whare Tapa Whā is a Māori model that focuses on four key areas of well-being: physical, spiritual, mental, and family health.

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

Focusing on the physical health and being fit is as important as mental health. Having a healty body makes you energised and happy. It allows us to enjoy spending time with friends and hobbies. Being fit provides us with better quality of life and we can live a long life.

Taha Hinengaro (emotional and mental wellbeing)

It is very crucial to maintain the mental well-being. Focusing on it can help to cope with life's challenges. Having positive emotions help us build stronger relationships. Being mentally and emotionally healthy helps in better achieving our goals. Positive mental health helps us in enjoying and appreciating good things in life.

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

Spiritual well-being is all about finding purpose and meaning in life. It helps in guiding challenges like a compass and also helps us in connecting with yourself, people around you and even nature. Spiritual health can be through meditation, religion, nature or simply reflecting on your values. Spiritual health can bring us inner peace, hope, sense of belonging and strength. It is something that gives your life a meaning by being connected to something.

Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

Family well-being is very important as it affects everyone in your family not just you. When the family supports and takes care of each other, you will find a sense of belonging and the individuals can thrive because of the safe and nurturing environment that was being created. Not just the family, family well-being includes friends and extended family. Their care and support can give you greater sense of purpose, stronger relationships and most importantly mental health.

Holistic Well-being Plan:

Wall Activities
Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing) Going to walk every evening, and when the weather is bad going to walk on tread mill.
Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing) Watching funny videos in youtube with my family during dinner and having conversations.
Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing) Listening to headspace meditation for 15 minutes when ever i am stressed.
Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing) Praying every morning to keep my friends and family safe.
Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment) Going on hikes or beach whenever I'm free.

Summary:

To create a healthy lifestyle, consider these areas:

Physical health: Eat well, exercise, and get enough sleep.

Mental health: Manage stress and seek support when needed.

Spiritual health: Connect with your beliefs and values.

Family health: Build strong relationships and involve your family in your goals.

By focusing on all these areas, you can create a balanced and fulfilling life.
