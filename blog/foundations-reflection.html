Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

  • Ego's Hindrance: My ego can get in the way of learning. Sometimes, my ego makes me afraid to try new things or make mistakes. This can slow down my learning process.

  • The Value of Empathy: Empathy is crucial for understanding other's viewpoints and building strong relationships. By putting myself in other people's shoes, I can better understand their needs and work with them more effectively. It's especially important in the collaborative world of programming.

  • Self-Awareness is Key: Recognizing my strengths and weaknesses helps me identify areas where I can improve. This makes me a better learner and problem-solver.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

  • Values: Our values guide our actions and decisions. In programming, strong values like integrity, honesty, and teamwork are essential for building ethical and reliable software.

  • Empathy: Empathy helps us understand our users' needs and perspectives. This enables us to create products that are not only functional but also user-friendly and accessible.

  • Self-Awareness: Self-awareness is crucial for effective communication and collaboration. It helps us recognize our own biases and limitations, and work towards improving them.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

The most surprising thing I've learned about human skills was how much they connect to programming. I didn't realize that soft skills like empathy and communication are just as important as technical skills when making good software.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

Overcoming my own ego and biases was the most challenging aspect. It's not always easy to admit my mistakes or be open to feedback. However, I've learned that these challenges are essential for growth and improvement.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on Human Skills learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Focusing on human skills is essential for a successful developer. While technical skills are important, being able to work well with others, understand user needs, and communicate clearly is just as crucial. It helps you collaborate effectively in teams and create products that people actually want to use.

Does the time you spent studying Human Skills learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

No, the time spent studying Human Skills learning was not a waste. In fact, I believe it was a valuable investment. While technical skills are essential, soft skills are equally important for success in the programming field. These skills will help me build stronger relationships, communicate more effectively, and become a more valuable team member.
